HOW A SHOOT WORKS

THE PROCESS

We talk about the car, the look you want, and pick a time when the light's actually right — usually early morning or the last two hours before sunset.
I pick a backdrop that fits the car — industrial, rural, downtown — not whatever parking lot is closest.
45–90 minutes on site. Multiple angles, low glass shots, and detail passes on the wheels, badges, and interior.
I cull down to the frames that actually hit — usually 15–25 keepers out of a few hundred shots.
Each photo is graded by hand to match the car's paint and the light it was shot in — no one-click presets.
Full-resolution files delivered digitally, sized for Instagram, your build thread, or print.
